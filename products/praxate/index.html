What is CCTV workforce intelligence, and why does it matter now?

For two decades, CCTV has been a passive recording device — the thing you check after something goes wrong. Open-source computer vision has finally caught up with the hardware. Now your existing cameras can be sensors: attendance, headcount, PPE compliance, dwell time, intrusion — measured in real time, not in hindsight.

Praxate is the layer that makes this practical for Indian factories, offices, warehouses, schools, and clinics — without ripping out your DVR or sending your video to someone else's cloud.

What it does

One platform, several things your team currently pays three vendors to do.

Face-based attendance

No biometric reader, no card-swipe queue. Employees walk in, the camera recognizes them, attendance is logged. Zero touch, zero hardware, zero training.

Headcount & occupancy

How many people are in zone A right now? How many came through gate 2 today? Real-time counts, hourly trends, occupancy heatmaps.

PPE detection

Helmet, vest, mask, gloves — flag workers entering hazard zones without required PPE. Auditable compliance records for safety officers and regulators.

Intrusion & after-hours alerts

Define zones and time windows. Someone in the warehouse at 2am? WhatsApp alert to the manager in 5 seconds. With the video clip attached.

Loitering & dwell time

Track how long people stay in a zone — useful for queue management, productivity analysis, and spotting unauthorized loitering.

One dashboard, all sites

Multi-location, role-based access, exports to HRMS/ERP. Plant manager sees their plant. HR sees company-wide attendance. Owner sees everything.

Common questions

What buyers ask on every first call.

What is CCTV workforce intelligence?

It's the practice of running AI on existing CCTV camera feeds to extract operational and HR insights — attendance from face, headcount, PPE compliance, loitering, intrusion. Instead of cameras being passive recording devices for after-the-fact review, they become real-time sensors for the operations team.

What is Praxate?

Praxate is an AI platform that turns existing CCTV cameras into workforce intelligence. It runs face-based attendance, headcount, PPE detection, intrusion alerts, and workforce analytics on your existing IP cameras — with local on-prem processing, so no video ever leaves your premises.

Do we need to replace our CCTV cameras?

No. Praxate works on existing IP cameras that support RTSP — the standard for any modern CCTV install in India. We connect to your DVR/NVR or directly to cameras over the network. No new hardware in 95% of cases.

Where is the video processed?

Locally, on a small Praxate appliance at your site. Video never leaves your premises. Only the structured events (attendance, alerts, headcount) are synced to your dashboard. This satisfies most privacy, IT and labour-law concerns out of the box.

Is face-based attendance legal in India?

Yes, when employees are notified and consent is documented — which Praxate has built-in workflows for. For factories and offices already running CCTV, this is generally a smaller compliance lift than biometric fingerprint systems. We can help you draft the right consent and signage.

How accurate is the face-based attendance?

On well-positioned cameras (entrance / clock-in zone) at decent resolution, Praxate achieves 97%+ recognition accuracy. We profile every site before rollout — if a camera angle is wrong, we tell you what to move before signing anyone up.

How is Praxate different from other AI CCTV products?

Two things. First, Praxate is fully on-prem — your video never goes to the cloud, which matters for most Indian factories and offices. Second, it's built workforce-first, not security-first — so attendance, PPE and headcount are first-class capabilities, not bolted on after motion detection.

How is it priced?

Per-camera per-month, with volume discounts and a one-time on-site appliance cost. We're in early access — pilot pricing is available for the first wave of sites. Talk to us for a quote tailored to your camera count and site layout.
