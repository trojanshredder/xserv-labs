# Xserv + Traxium Content Strategy

**Goal:** 1M LinkedIn impressions over 12 months
**Audience:** logistics operators, manufacturers, hospitality GMs, midmarket CTOs, US/UK/UAE procurement teams
**Real metric we care about:** inbound DMs from buyers, not vanity impressions

---

## Why we are doing this

For a decade we have built quietly, attracted clients via referral, and never told the broader market what we actually do. That model has a ceiling. To get past the ceiling, we need consistent, useful, opinionated content that signals to buyers what kind of team we are. The goal is not influence. The goal is the right inbound.

## Engagement rhythm (where 50% of impressions actually come from)

**Within 30 minutes of any post going live:**
- Distribution Lead alerts team in the dedicated channel
- 3-5 team members like and add real comments (not "great post")
- Founder DMs the post to 2-3 relevant external contacts

**Daily, 15 minutes per team member:**
- Each voice comments on 5 big accounts in their domain
  - Lead Voice: CTOs, fleet ops leaders, hospitality tech voices
  - Engineering Voice: open-source maintainers, well-known engineers
  - Product Voice: logistics and fleet ops accounts

That is 600-2,400 free impressions per day from engagement work alone.

---

## Content sourcing — never run out of ideas

Build a rolling content bank. Sources:

1. **Client interactions** — every call, every bug, every weird question is a potential post
2. **Engineering wins and losses** — deploys, debugging stories, refactors
3. **Industry news** — one reaction per week with a real opinion
4. **Behind-the-scenes** — whiteboards, deploys, team moments
5. **Repurposing** — every text post becomes a carousel 6 weeks later

**Monday 15-minute content meeting:** decide the week's 5-7 pieces, assign owners. Done.

## What we track weekly (not daily)

| Metric | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Impressions per post | What is working |
| Comments per post | Best leading indicator of reach |
| New followers per week | Audience growth |
| Profile views per week | Top of inbound funnel |
| **Inbound DMs from buyers** | The metric that actually pays the bills |

A week with 200K impressions and zero buyer DMs is a failure. A week with 5K impressions and 2 buyer DMs is a win.

## First-week actions

1. Founder sends the kickoff message and books a 30-min Monday meeting
2. Team agrees roles and confirms time commitment
3. Distribution Lead sets up Buffer/Hypefury with all 3 accounts (founder personal, Xserv page, Traxium page)
4. Distribution Lead creates the shared content doc (Notion or Google Doc) with weekly schedule template
5. Define the engagement Slack/WhatsApp channel
6. Pick the first 5 posts for week 1 from existing draft inventory
7. Schedule them
8. Run them

## Ground rules

- Every post types out in LinkedIn directly rather than pasting from a doc. Small edits while typing make the prose more human.
- Banned phrases (these read as AI-formulaic and have been overused): "ten years," "a decade," "have learned," "the hardest part," "honest reflection," "mostly the unglamorous parts," "it is the part of the work that..." — pick fresh phrasing.
- Each post needs one real point. If you cannot say it in one sentence, do not post it.
- No engagement bait ("Agree?" or "Thoughts?" or "Comment X if you Y"). Real opinions, real questions, no manipulation.
- No buying followers, no engagement pods, no comment swaps. The audience compounds slowly but it compounds with the right people.
