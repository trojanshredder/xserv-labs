# Week 4 video script — "What surprised me most about running a software studio"

**Length target:** 45 seconds
**Format:** Selfie video
**Account:** Founder personal

---

## Script

```
The thing that has surprised me most about running a software studio is how little of the job is actually software.

I thought, naively, that the work would mostly be writing code and managing engineers. Maybe a third of my week, even now, is spent on that.

The rest is conversation. Conversations with clients about what they actually need. Conversations with the team about how we are going to deliver it. Conversations with vendors about why their API is broken. Conversations with prospects who are not sure whether to hire us yet. Conversations with my cofounder about the next thing.

The companies that survive in this business are the ones whose founders enjoy these conversations, or at minimum tolerate them well. The technical people who get into this work because they want to be in the code mostly leave within two or three years to be in the code again.

There is no shortcut around the conversations. If you are starting a service business in software, the sooner you make peace with that, the better.

That's it.
```
