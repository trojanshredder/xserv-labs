# Week 3 video script — "When to migrate off Excel"

**Length target:** 45 seconds
**Format:** Selfie video
**Account:** Founder personal

---

## Script

```
A question I get asked a lot by founders running operations on Excel: when do we stop?

My answer: stop when the spreadsheet has more than 5 people writing to it at the same time, or when nobody on the team can tell you what is in cell K12 without checking.

That is it. Those are the two signs.

Before those signs, Excel is fine. Genuinely fine. Half the businesses we have worked with were running profitably on Excel for years. The pressure to "modernise" before those signs hit is usually coming from somebody who is selling you something.

After those signs, the cost of staying on the spreadsheet starts to outweigh the cost of building something proper. People stop trusting the data. Decisions get slower. The same calculation gets re-done five times a week by five different people because nobody trusts the one in the sheet.

If you are not at those two signs yet, keep using Excel and stop apologising for it.

That's it.
```
