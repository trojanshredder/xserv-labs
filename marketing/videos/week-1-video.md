# Week 1 video script — "One thing I'd tell anyone evaluating an ERP"

**Length target:** 45 seconds
**Format:** Selfie video, phone propped on desk, single take
**Account:** Founder personal

---

## Script (read at normal pace)

```
If you're about to sign with an ERP vendor — Opera, SAP, Oracle, IDS Next, anyone — there's one question to ask that most people skip.

It's: "What happens in the first thirty days after go-live, and who pays for it?"

Because every ERP rollout has a rough first month. Workflows that did not show up in discovery. Data that does not migrate cleanly. People in your team who refuse to use it for the first two weeks. That stuff is universal. It is not the vendor's fault and it is not your fault. It just happens.

The question is whether the vendor has budgeted for it on their side, or whether you are about to discover that "go-live support" means you call them and they bill you.

Ask. Get it in writing. The difference between a vendor who has built that into their pricing and one who has not is the difference between a smooth rollout and a six-month war.

That's it.
```

---

## Filming notes

- Phone propped horizontally on a stack of books at eye level
- Natural light from a window in front of you (not behind)
- Look at the phone lens, not the screen
- Talk slightly faster than feels natural (LinkedIn viewers watch at higher speed)
- Don't script word-for-word — read it through twice, then say it your own way
- If you stumble, keep going. Tiny imperfections make it feel real.
- Length should land at 40-55 seconds. If over 60, cut the "Because every ERP rollout..." sentence.
