## Order of operations

### This week, before starting

1. Read `content-strategy.md`. Pick the people for the roles. Confirm they are in.
2. Edit `team-kickoff-message.md` with their actual names. Send it.
3. Schedule a 30-minute kickoff meeting on Monday.
4. In the meeting, walk through the strategy doc + master plan together.
5. Set up Buffer or Hypefury with all 3 accounts (Founder personal, Xserv company, Traxium company).
6. Create the engagement channel (Slack or WhatsApp group named `#content-launch` or similar).
7. Designate the Distribution Lead. They own scheduling and engagement.

### Week 1 (and every week)

1. **Monday morning:** 15-minute content meeting. Confirm the week's 5-7 posts. Assign owners.
2. **Across the week:** owners finalise their drafts (most are already written in `posts/` and `carousels/` and `videos/`). Distribution Lead schedules them in Buffer.
3. **Each morning a post drops:** Distribution Lead pings the team channel. Team likes + comments within 30 minutes.
4. **Within an hour of each post:** Founder DMs 3-5 strategic contacts (template in `engagement-templates.md`).
5. **Every day:** each team member spends 15 minutes commenting on bigger accounts in their domain.
6. **Friday afternoon:** 15-minute review. What worked, what flopped, what to adjust for next week.

## Goal metrics (review monthly, not daily)

- Monthly impressions
- Comments per post
- New followers per month
- Profile views per month
- **Inbound DMs from buyers** (the metric that pays the bills)

Realistic 12-month target: 1M cumulative impressions, ~50-100 inbound buyer DMs.

---

## When to deviate from the plan

The schedule is a backbone, not a contract. Substitute any planned post if:

- A real client moment happens that is worth writing about
- An industry news event lands that you have an opinion on
- A competitor does something noteworthy
- Someone on the team has a strong post idea

Just keep the cadence (5-7 posts per week) and the format mix (text + carousel + video).

---

## When something works unexpectedly well

- If a post hits 10x your normal impressions, capture what made it work in a note. Pattern goes into next month's plan.
- If a piece of content drives 3+ inbound DMs from real buyers, repost a variation 4-6 weeks later. Top-of-funnel content gets recycled aggressively.

## Authors

These drafts were prepared as a starting kit. Adapt them. Replace examples with your own real ones where possible. Specifics from real life always outperform inherited ones.

The brand voice has been kept consistent across all 28 pieces of content:
- No "ten years," "decade," "learned," "the hardest part" — these became formulaic
- Specific examples over abstractions
- No fake confidence — real opinions including their limits
- Casual sentence-length variance; not every paragraph has the same rhythm
- Closers are usually direct, sometimes flat, never a tricolon

Keep that voice as the team writes new content. The drafts are templates for tone as much as for topics.
