# Week 3 carousel — "Hiring engineers: what we actually look for"

**Account:** Xserv company page
**Format:** PDF carousel
**Slide count:** 9 slides

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Text:**
```
HIRING ENGINEERS

What we actually look for at Xserv Labs.

(It is not what is on the resume.)
```

---

## Slide 2 — Point 1

**Text:**
```
1.
Patience under uncertainty.

Show them a piece of unfamiliar code with a bug. Watch what they do. The ones who slow down, read it carefully, ask questions before guessing — those are the ones to hire.

Speed is overrated. Calibration is not.
```

---

## Slide 3 — Point 2

**Text:**
```
2.
The ability to say "I do not know."

Most candidates feel pressure to have an answer for everything in an interview. The senior engineers we have hired all said "I do not know" at least twice during their interview.

Saying I do not know is a skill. We hire for it.
```

---

## Slide 4 — Point 3

**Text:**
```
3.
Curiosity about the business, not just the code.

We work in logistics, manufacturing, hospitality, finance. Candidates who ask "what does this company actually do" within the first thirty minutes — those are the engineers who build the right thing.

Candidates who ask only about the tech stack — those build elegant things that nobody uses.
```

---

## Slide 5 — Point 4

**Text:**
```
4.
Comfort writing things that are not code.

A status update. A design document. A message to a client. An onboarding doc.

Engineers who hate writing produce technical work that requires other people to translate. Engineers who can write are 2x more useful at every level.
```

---

## Slide 6 — Point 5

**Text:**
```
5.
How they talk about their last team.

Were they specific and fair about what worked and what did not? Or did they badmouth everyone they had ever worked with?

How a person talks about their last team is how they will eventually talk about yours.
```

---

## Slide 7 — Point 6

**Text:**
```
6.
What they have built that is theirs.

Not a project from work. Something they made on their own time, however small. A side project. A blog. A tiny library. A spreadsheet model for something they care about.

The engineers who build things outside of being paid to build things — those people stay sharp over years.
```

---

## Slide 8 — What we DO NOT screen for

**Text:**
```
WHAT WE DO NOT SCREEN FOR

- The college on the resume
- Years of experience in the exact stack
- LeetCode performance
- Whether they have worked at a "big" company
- Speed of typing in interviews

None of these have predicted long-term success in our team.
```
