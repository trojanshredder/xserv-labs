---

## Slide 2 — Day 1 morning

**Text:**
```
DAY 1
Morning

Walk the floor. Talk to the people who will actually use the software, not the people commissioning it.

The two are usually different. The gap between what one wants and what the other needs is where most projects go wrong.
```

---

## Slide 3 — Day 1 afternoon

**Text:**
```
DAY 1
Afternoon

Watch the workflow. Not "explain the workflow to us." Watch it.

What people actually do is rarely what they say they do. Tools they use, shortcuts they have invented, the WhatsApp groups that hold things together. None of this shows up in a meeting.
```

---

## Slide 4 — Day 2

**Text:**
```
DAY 2

Map the data.

Where does it live now (Excel, paper, someone's head, three different systems). Who enters it. Who corrects it when it is wrong. What "wrong" even means in this context.

Most projects fail at the data layer, not the application layer.
```

---

## Slide 5 — Day 3

**Text:**
```
DAY 3

Sit with the back office.

The accounts team. The dispatch team. The compliance person. The reception. They see things the leadership does not see.

Almost every "we did not see this coming" production issue was visible to someone in the back office for months.
```

---

## Slide 6 — Day 4

**Text:**
```
DAY 4

Find the workarounds.

The Excel sheet that runs the actual business. The script someone wrote in 2019 that nobody dares touch. The manual export that happens at 6 PM every Friday.

These are not problems to eliminate. They are the system, currently held together by people. Software has to replace them, not ignore them.
```

---

## Slide 7 — Day 5

**Text:**
```
DAY 5

Decide what NOT to build.

Most projects ship 100% of the requested features and 60% of the value. The 40% gap is features that should not have been built.

By the end of week one, we want to know which features to push back on.
```

---

## Slide 8 — Output

**Text:**
```
THE OUTPUT

A one-page document the client and the team agree on, before any code:

What we will build
Why
What we will explicitly NOT build (and why)
What the first usable version looks like in 4-6 weeks
```

## Slide 9 — Cost

**Text:**
```
WHY THIS MATTERS

A discovery week typically costs ₹2-4 lakh.
A wrong-scoped project costs ₹40 lakh+ and 9 months of pain.

The math is obvious. The reason most projects skip discovery is that nobody is patient enough to spend a week looking before they start typing.
```
