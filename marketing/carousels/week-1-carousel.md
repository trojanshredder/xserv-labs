## Slide 2 — Red flag #1

**Text:**
```
1.
"Fixed price, fixed scope, fixed timeline."

You cannot have all three. The vendors who promise all three are charging you for risk they have not yet uncovered, or they are about to learn things mid-project that will mean a renegotiation. Often a painful one.

The honest version: fix two, leave one flexible.
```

**Design:** Number "1." large in orange. Body text in white on dark.

---

## Slide 3 — Red flag #2

**Text:**
```
2.
The proposal lists technologies, not outcomes.

If the proposal tells you React, Node, Postgres, AWS, and microservices, but never tells you what the business will be able to do that it cannot do today, the vendor is selling implementation, not solution.

You want a vendor who tells you what changes for your team in month 2.
```

---

## Slide 4 — Red flag #3

**Text:**
```
3.
The team profile shows senior names you will never meet.

Most agencies put the senior engineers on the sales call and the junior team on your project. Ask specifically who will be on your project in month 4. Get the names. If they cannot tell you, you are about to hire a junior team.
```

---

## Slide 5 — Red flag #4

**Text:**
```
4.
"We will define detailed requirements in phase 1."

This is fine if the vendor has scoped the budget for that phase realistically. It is a red flag if the rest of the budget is a fixed price that depends on requirements they have not yet seen.

Phase 1 should have a separate, smaller commitment. Then re-quote.
```

---

## Slide 6 — Red flag #5

**Text:**
```
5.
No mention of who owns the code, the data, or the deployment infrastructure.

If this is not explicit in writing, assume the vendor will own it. That means you are renting your own system from them, indefinitely, with no exit.

Get the ownership in writing before signing.
```

---

## Slide 7 — Wrap

**Text:**
```
None of these are deal-breakers on their own.

But if you see two or more, slow down. Ask harder questions. The right vendor will welcome the scrutiny.

The wrong vendor will rush you toward signing.
```
