# Engagement templates

Ready-to-paste templates for the engagement work that drives 50% of post reach. The Distribution Lead should keep this file open and use it for every post.

---

## 1. The team-channel ping (when a post goes live)

Send this in the dedicated team channel (Slack or WhatsApp) within 5 minutes of any post going live.

```
🚀 New post live: [link]

Quick asks:
1. Like the post (takes 5 seconds)
2. Add a real comment in the next 30 min (one sentence is fine — what stood out, a question, a related experience)
3. If it resonates, repost or share with someone who would care

Engagement in the first hour determines whether 200 people see this or 20,000. Two minutes of your day, big impact on reach.

Thanks team.
```

---

## 2. Team member comment starters

Avoid "Great post 🔥" and "Well said." LinkedIn's algorithm treats these as low-quality. Use real responses. Templates below for when team members do not know what to say.

**If the post was a story:**
```
Have run into a version of this myself with [specific situation]. The [specific detail in post] is exactly right.
```

**If the post was an opinion:**
```
Curious whether you have seen [specific edge case] where this does not hold. We have had one or two of those at [team/company].
```

**If the post was a how-to or checklist:**
```
Adding to this — [one specific addition or experience] is something we have started doing for the same reason.
```

**If the post was a product highlight (Traxium):**
```
[Specific question about how the feature handles a real-world edge case]
```

The key: every comment should have one specific detail. No generic encouragements. The algorithm and the audience both know the difference.

## 3. Founder DM script (post-publish outreach)

Within the first hour of any major post, the founder should DM 3-5 people who would specifically care. Use this template, customised per person.

```
Hey [Name],

Just wrote something about [topic] that I think you might find useful — [link].

The [specific point relevant to them, e.g., "third point about discovery weeks" or "the bit about vendor lock-in"] reminded me of [something specific you remember about their work or situation].

Would love your take if you have a second. Either way, hope all is well.

[Your name]
```

Rules:
- Send to 3-5 people only
- Pick people who would genuinely care, not random connections
- Reference something specific you know about them or their work
- Do not say "please like and share." That is begging. Just send the link and a sincere ask for their take.

## 4. First comment template (for posts with external links)

Posts with external URLs in the body get throttled by LinkedIn's algorithm. So we put the link in the first comment instead.

**For Traxium posts:**
```
For anyone who wants to see Traxium in action or sign up for the free first month: https://www.traxium.in
```

**For Xserv posts with external resources:**
```
Full resource is at https://xservlabs.com — happy to DM the longer version of this if useful.
```

**For thought leadership posts (no link to drop, but engagement push):**
```
Curious what others have run into. Reply with your version of this story if you have one.
```

---

## 5. Replying to comments (founder)

Reply to every comment in the first hour. The algorithm pushes posts that have ongoing conversation.

**Templates for common comments:**

If they agree with the post:
```
Yeah, [specific echo of their point]. The [thing they mentioned] is the one that catches most people out.
```

If they disagree or push back:
```
Fair point. The case for [their counter-position] is real, especially in [specific context]. Where I would still push back is [specific reason]. Curious where you have landed on this.
```

If they share their own story:
```
That sounds familiar. [Specific detail you noticed in their story]. Did you end up [follow-up question]?
```

If they ask a question:
Just answer it. Briefly. Add one more sentence of context. Done.

Avoid:
- "Thanks for sharing!" alone
- Generic agreement
- Long answers that read like another post

## 6. The follow-up message (1 week later)

For people who engaged meaningfully on a post (commented thoughtfully, shared, sent a DM), send a follow-up a week later.

```
Hey [Name],

Saw your comment on last week's post about [topic]. Wanted to thank you for engaging properly with it — most people just like and scroll.

Curious what you are working on these days. Always happy to compare notes.

[Your name]
```

This is how a content strategy turns into actual business relationships. Most people skip it. Skipping it is leaving the highest-value step of the entire pipeline on the table.

## 7. Daily commenting schedule (per team member)

Each role spends 15 minutes a day commenting on bigger accounts in their domain. Each comment should be substantive (3-4 sentences) and reference something specific from the post being commented on.

**Founder targets to follow + comment:**
- Notable founders in software services and B2B SaaS (Indian and global)
- CTOs at midmarket companies you would want as clients
- Industry voices in logistics, manufacturing, hospitality

**Engineering Voice targets:**
- Open-source maintainers
- Engineering managers at companies you respect
- Authors of technical blog posts and books

**Product Voice (Traxium) targets:**
- Fleet ops leaders
- Logistics company founders and executives
- Indian fleet tech accounts

The goal of each comment is not to get the post author to notice you. It is for the post author's followers to click your profile.

---

## 8. What NOT to do

- No buying followers
- No engagement pods or comment-swap groups
- No "Agree?" or "Comment X if you Y" engagement bait
- No tagging random influencers in your posts hoping for reshares
- No commenting on competitors' posts to badmouth them
- No DMing the founder of a 500K-follower account asking them to reshare your post

These things either do not work, work briefly and then backfire, or get the account flagged. The slow strategy compounds. The shortcuts do not.
