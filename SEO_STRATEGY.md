## 0. Honest Baseline & Strategic Premise

A quick reality check before tactics:

1. **You will not outrank TCS, Infosys, Accenture, or Clutch/GoodFirms** for head terms like "software development company India." Don't try. Those SERPs are owned by directories (Clutch, GoodFirms, DesignRush, TechBehemoths, The Manifest, SelectedFirms) and Fortune 500 enterprise pages. The winning move is to *get listed inside those directories at the top* and *own the long-tail and mid-tail commercial queries* where buying intent is high and CPC is real.
2. **Branded SERP** ("xserv labs", "xservlabs", "traxium", "praxate") is winnable in 30 days because there's almost no competition for those terms. This is the cheapest win available — do it first.
3. **2026 search is bifurcated:** classic blue links + Google AI Overviews. AI Overviews cite pages already ranking in positions 1-10 (Search Engine Land), so your job remains "rank in the top 10" — but the *content structure* (heading + 1-3 declarative sentences below it) determines whether you get pulled into AIO. Build for both.
4. **The most underused lever for a 10-year-old studio:** case studies + product-led content + verified third-party reviews. You have Traxium and Praxate — use them.

## 4. Technical SEO Checklist (Beyond the Basics)

### Core Web Vitals (priority order for 2026)

1. **INP (Interaction to Next Paint) ≤ 200ms** — 43% of sites fail this; most commonly failed CWV in 2026. React/Next.js sites particularly vulnerable. Audit: PageSpeed Insights → field data. Fix: break up long tasks, debounce event handlers, lazy-load heavy JS, use `useTransition` for non-urgent updates.
2. **LCP ≤ 2.5s** — Confirmed ranking signal. Failing LCP caps rankings regardless of content quality. Fix: preload hero image with `<link rel="preload">`, serve hero in AVIF/WebP at right dimensions, eliminate render-blocking JS above the fold, use a real CDN (Cloudflare/Fastly), use `fetchpriority="high"` on hero.
3. **CLS ≤ 0.1** — set explicit width/height on every image and video element; reserve space for ads, embeds, and font-loaded text.
4. Test on **real Indian mobile conditions** — mid-range Android on 4G. WebPageTest with Moto G4 + Slow 4G profile.

### Image optimization

- AVIF first, WebP fallback, JPEG as last resort.
- Responsive images via `<picture>` with `srcset`.
- Squoosh for one-offs, sharp/imagemin in build pipeline.
- Descriptive filenames (`traxium-fleet-dashboard-hyderabad.avif`, not `IMG_4521.jpg`).
- Alt text: descriptive, not keyword-stuffed. Decorative → `alt=""`.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold (`loading="lazy"`), eager-load LCP image.

### Internal linking — hub-and-spoke

- **Homepage** → all 8 service hubs, both products, all 5 industry hubs.
- **Service hub** → industry variants, related services, 3-5 supporting blog posts.
- **Industry hub** → every service offered for that industry + relevant case studies.
- **Blog post** → 2-3 related blog posts + 1-2 service/product pages with descriptive anchor text.
- Breadcrumbs everywhere with `BreadcrumbList` schema.
- Aim: every important page reachable within 3 clicks from homepage.

### Schema types

- `Organization` ✅ — include `sameAs`, `address`, `contactPoint`, `founder`, `foundingDate: 2015`.
- `LocalBusiness` ✅ (subtype: `ProfessionalService`) with `geo` and `openingHoursSpecification`.
- `Service` on every service page with `provider`, `areaServed`, `serviceType`, `offers`.
- `SoftwareApplication` ✅ on Traxium and Praxate pages.
- `Product` + `AggregateRating` once Clutch reviews exist (real data only).
- `FAQPage` on every service and pricing page.
- `HowTo` on tutorial blog posts.
- `Article` + `Person` (author) on every blog post.
- `BreadcrumbList` site-wide.
- `VideoObject` if you add founder-led explainer videos.

### Indexability traps

- Faceted nav / filters generating thousands of URLs — block with `robots.txt` or `noindex,follow`.
- Internal search pages — `noindex`.
- Staging subdomain — HTTP auth + robots block.
- Trailing slash inconsistency — pick one, 301 the other.
- WWW vs non-WWW — pick one canonical.
- `hreflang` — skip for now. When you launch /us/ /uk/ subfolders use `x-default` + `en-IN`, `en-US`, `en-GB`.
- Pagination — proper self-canonicals, not canonical to page 1.
- Soft 404s — return real 404.
- JS rendering — confirm Googlebot can render. Test via URL Inspection. SSR/pre-render critical pages if client-side only.

### Other wins

- **XML sitemap segmentation:** separate `services-sitemap.xml`, `products-sitemap.xml`, `blog-sitemap.xml`, `industries-sitemap.xml`. Submit each to GSC.
- Last-modified dates in sitemap — update when content meaningfully changes.
- Robots.txt: explicitly allow JS/CSS resource paths.
- Structured data testing: every template through Schema.org validator + GSC Rich Results test before pushing.
- HTTP/2 or HTTP/3 server.
- Brotli compression beyond gzip.

## 9. Things to Push Back On / Don't Do

- **Don't buy DA-X backlinks from Fiverr/Upwork.** Worth less than zero. Algorithmic penalty risk is real and lasting.
- **Don't generate 500 programmatic pages on day 1.** Google's helpful content system specifically targets this. Ship 10, prove they index and earn impressions, scale.
- **Don't AI-generate blog content end-to-end.** Use AI to draft, but founder/expert must edit substantively. AI Overviews actively de-rank generic-feeling content.
- **Don't chase "software development company india"** as a primary target. Win the long-tail + Hyderabad + product queries; they convert better anyway.
- **Don't skip the review acquisition work.** Single highest-leverage SEO + conversion action available, most agencies neglect it.
- **Don't redesign the site mid-strategy.** The recent redesign needs 6 months to mature.

---

## 10. The One Thing That Matters Most

**Publish 8 deeply technical, original case studies in 90 days — one per major service/vertical — each containing real metrics, real client names (with permission), and an actual architecture diagram. Then promote each one hard on LinkedIn through the founder's account and via HARO/Qwoted commentary.**

That single asset class compounds: it earns links (engineers and journalists cite real numbers), it ranks (long-tail intent-rich queries), it feeds AI Overviews (declarative facts with attribution), it converts (proof beats positioning), and it differentiates you from 95% of Indian software studios who publish brochure-grade case studies.

Everything else is supporting infrastructure for that.
