## 2. Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools verification

### Google Search Console

1. Go to https://search.google.com/search-console
2. Add property → choose **Domain property** (preferred): `xservlabs.com`
3. Verify via DNS TXT record (Cloudflare/GoDaddy/your DNS provider). Or URL prefix: `https://xservlabs.com/` and use the meta tag method (paste into [index.html](index.html) head).
4. Once verified:
   - **Sitemaps** → submit `https://xservlabs.com/sitemap.xml`
   - **URL Inspection** → request indexing for homepage
   - **URL Inspection** → request indexing for each of the top 5 highest-priority pages:
     - `/services/erp-software-development/`
     - `/services/custom-software-development/`
     - `/products/traxium/`
     - `/products/praxate/`
     - `/blog/cost-to-build-erp-in-india/`

### Bing Webmaster Tools

1. Go to https://www.bing.com/webmasters
2. Easiest path: "Import from Google Search Console" — once GSC is set up
3. Submit the same sitemap URL

### After both verifications

- [ ] Add Bing & Google verification meta tags (if not already present) to [index.html](index.html) head
- [ ] Set up email alerts for indexing errors and security issues

## 5. HARO / Qwoted / Featured pitch templates

### Active platforms (May 2026)

- **Featured.com** (owns HARO) — primary, daily monitoring
- **Qwoted** — higher-quality B2B queries, free plan 2 pitches/month
- **Source of Sources (SOS)** — free, sosfor.com
- **Help a B2B Writer** (now MentionMatch via Superpath) — most relevant for XServ
- **#JournoRequest on X / Twitter** — fastest turnaround

### Daily workflow

- 20 minutes/day, max 3 pitches/day
- Each pitch is tied to a founder/CTO bio link to xservlabs.com/about (or homepage)
- Track sent pitches and placements in a simple spreadsheet
- Target: 4–6 placements/month

### Pitch template — for technical / industry expertise

**Subject (matches journalist's request exactly):** [paste their request title]

```

We ship our own products (Traxium, Praxate) and build custom platforms for clients across India, the US, and the UAE.

Here's a take you can use, attribute or paraphrase:

"[2–3 sentence opinion / insight that's specific, slightly contrarian, and directly answers their query. Include a real-world data point or example from work you've done.]"

Happy to expand if useful, or jump on a 15-minute call if you'd prefer. Bio and prior writing here: https://xservlabs.com/

Thanks for the chance to contribute,
[Your Name]
[Title]
XServ Labs · Hyderabad
```

### Pitch template — for data / India-specific stats

```
Hi [Journalist Name],

Replying to your [topic] request.

I'm the founder of XServ Labs — we build software for Indian fleet, manufacturing, and SaaS companies. We have first-party data on [relevant metric they're asking about], based on [number] deployments / [type of clients] over the past [timeframe].

Sharing one data point you can attribute:

"[Specific, surprising, India-context data point. e.g.: 'Across the 200+ Indian SME fleets we have observed on our platform, fuel pilferage detection alone recovers 5-12% of fuel spend in the first 90 days — typically ₹50,000-₹2 lakh per month per fleet of 50 trucks.']"

Happy to send the full data view or jump on a quick call if helpful.

Bio: https://xservlabs.com/
LinkedIn: [your LinkedIn]

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### What makes pitches land

- Reply within 2 hours of the request going out
- Personalise the first line — proves you read the request
- Give them something usable in the email itself, not just "happy to chat"
- Specific numbers > vague claims
- Bio + link in signature, never as the lead
- Don't pitch every request — only the ones where you genuinely have a specific take

## 9. Case study interview script

When a client agrees to a case study, run a 30-minute conversation to gather the material. Record (with permission) so you don't have to take notes.

### Questions to ask

**Context (5 min):**
1. What was the situation before you started working with us? What was hurting?
2. What had you tried before that didn't work?
3. Why did you pick us specifically over other options?

**Process (10 min):**
4. Walk me through how the engagement actually went. What stood out?
5. What did we get right?
6. Where did we struggle, and how did we handle it?
7. Was anything surprising — good or bad?

**Outcomes (10 min) — this is the SEO gold:**
8. What measurable changes did you see? (Numbers, percentages, time saved, money saved)
9. What changed for your team's day-to-day?
10. What's the business impact you can point to?
11. If a friend asked, what would you tell them about working with us?

**Permissions (5 min):**
12. Can we use your name + company name in the case study? Or do you want it anonymised?
13. Any photos we can use? (Product screenshots, your team using the platform, etc.)
14. Are there specific numbers you'd prefer we round or approximate?

### Writing the case study

- Lead with the most impressive outcome (specific number)
- Tell the story chronologically
- Use direct quotes from the interview (with permission)
- Include a "what we'd do differently" moment for credibility
- End with a forward-looking note
